# Glossary

A comprehensive glossary of terms and concepts central to the teachings of Neville Goddard.

---

## Abdullah
Abdullah was an Ethiopian rabbi and Neville Goddard's esoteric teacher. He introduced Neville to the deeper, psychological meanings of the Bible and the Kabbalah, teaching him the foundational principle that imagination creates reality. He instructed Neville through practical application, famously telling him, "You are already in Barbados" when Neville desired to travel there without money.

## Assumption
Assumption (or the Law of Assumption) is the act of adopting the feeling of the wish fulfilled. It is the practice of living *from* the end, assuming the psychological state of having already attained one's goal, rather than thinking *of* the goal. According to Neville, an assumption, though false, if persisted in, will harden into fact.

## Christ
In Neville's teaching, Christ is not a historical figure, but rather the Human Imagination itself. Christ is the creative power and wisdom of God embedded within every individual. The birth of Christ represents the awakening of man to the realization that his own imagination is God.

## Diet
Often referred to as a "mental diet," this is the practice of observing and controlling one's inner conversations and thoughts. Neville taught that just as our physical bodies are built from the food we eat, our external world is built from the internal words and ideas we consistently consume and entertain.

## Feeling
Feeling is the secret to manifestation. It is not merely an emotion, but the deep, sensory and psychological conviction that a desired state is already a present reality. To effectively impress the subconscious mind, a desire must be felt as a current fact.

## God
For Neville, God is not an external deity residing in the sky, but the absolute awareness of being, defined by the name "I AM." God and human imagination are one and the same. The external world is the outward projection of God (Imagination) expressing itself through man.

## I AM
"I AM" is the unconditioned awareness of being. It is the fundamental state of existence before any attributes (like "I am rich" or "I am poor") are attached to it. It is the true name of God and the core identity of every human being, serving as the blank canvas upon which reality is painted.

## Imagination
Human imagination is the divine body of the Lord Jesus Christ. It is the sole creator of the external world. Neville divided imagination into two forms: natural (carnal) imagination, which passively reflects the evidence of the senses, and Awakened Imagination, which actively dictates and creates reality from within.

## Revision
Revision is the practice of altering one's memory of past events to match what one *wishes* had happened. Neville taught that because time is an illusion and all things exist now in imagination, revising a past event effectively changes its consequences in the present and future.

## Sabbath
The Sabbath is not a specific day of the week, but a mystical state of mind. It is the profound sense of peace and rest that follows the successful assumption of a desire. When one has fully accepted that the work is done in imagination, they enter the Sabbath, resting from mental effort while the seed grows into physical manifestation.

## State
A state (or State of Consciousness) is an attitude of mind, a body of beliefs, or a psychological condition that a person occupies. Neville taught that there are infinite states of consciousness (like poverty, wealth, sickness, health) and that individuals are simply animating the state they currently reside in. A person can move from one state to another through imagination.

## The Law
The Law refers to the operational principle of the universe: "Imagining Creates Reality." It dictates that our outer world is an exact reflection of our inner state of consciousness. The Law can be consciously applied to change one's circumstances.

## The Promise
The Promise is the ultimate destiny of man: the awakening and unfolding of God within the individual. While The Law allows us to change our physical world, The Promise is the realization of our true divine nature, culminating in the complete resurrection of the individual as God.
